We are All in this Together

April 20, 2020 576 Views

While sheltering-in-place, my wife and I take daily four-mile walks through the hills east of San Francisco. Every day I marvel at the vast expanse of water flowing toward the majestic Pacific Ocean, the dramatic beauty of the Golden Gate Bridge and the soaring skyline of San Francisco. I’m still getting used to the new Salesforce Tower, a loud newcomer, who has pushed the Transamerica Pyramid to a supporting role.


During the first week, no walkers wore masks. People would pass on the sidewalk within a few feet of each other. As we moved into the second week and tragic deaths from Covid-19 climbed, social distancing became the norm. If we saw someone coming down the hill, a dance ensued where one of us would head into the street and walk along the parked cars to maintain a safe distance.


One neighbor keeps a day count posted on their front door. For Day 5, they had a “picket fence” - four lines with a diagonal slash across the lines. For Day 24, they had drawings of four dice, each showing the six side. For Day 30, they used Roman numerals: XXX. For Day 32, they wrote: “2x16” below the words, Math Matters.


Most walkers we pass have been warm and friendly. People have waved and said, “I’m smiling under my mask.” I’ve talked with people who are delivering groceries to elderly people who can’t leave their homes and with people who visit the sick and lonely. I've heard stories of people walking into a grocery store and paying for the people in line behind them.


We cut through a park every day and often see a musician sitting on a park bench strumming his guitar and singing Bob Dylan tunes.


Never have Dylan’s words struck me as more accurate than during these trying times:


Then you better start swimmin'
Or you'll sink like a stone
For the times they are a-changin'.


While sheltering-in-place, it’s easy to think you’re alone, but I’ve seen encouraging proof that we live in a community filled with caring people.
