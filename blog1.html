We are All in this Together

576 Views

My wife and I often walk through the hills east of San Francisco. Every day I marvel at the vast expanse of water flowing toward the majestic Pacific Ocean, the dramatic beauty of the Golden Gate Bridge and the soaring skyline of San Francisco. I’m still getting used to the new Salesforce Tower, a loud newcomer, who has pushed the Transamerica Pyramid to a supporting role.


At the beginning of sheltering-in-place months, no walkers wore masks. People would pass on the sidewalk within a few feet of each other. As we moved into the second week and tragic deaths from Covid-19 climbed, social distancing became the norm. If we saw someone coming down the hill, a dance ensued where one of us would head into the street and walk along the parked cars to maintain a safe distance.


One neighbor kept a day count posted on their front door. For Day 5, they had a “picket fence” - four lines with a diagonal slash across the lines. For Day 24, they had drawings of four dice, each showing the six side. For Day 30, they used Roman numerals: XXX. For Day 52, they pasted a deck of playing cards. For day 80, they had an "Around the World in 80 Days" sign. For Day 98, they wrote: “7 squared x 2” below the words, Math Matters. For day 108, they put up "10 squared plus 2 cubed."" For day 120, the posted a photo of a dozen eggs with "X 10" after it.


When shelter-in-place mandates ended, people are back to beign friendly. Those who still wear masks, often say, “I’m smiling under my mask.” I’ve talked with people who deliver groceries to elderly people who can’t leave their homes and with people who visit the sick and lonely. I've heard stories of people walking into a grocery store and paying for the people in line behind them.


We cut through a park every day and often see a musician sitting on a park bench strumming his guitar and singing Bob Dylan tunes.


Never have Dylan’s words struck me as more accurate than during these trying times:


Then you better start swimmin'
Or you'll sink like a stone
For the times they are a-changin'.


It’s easy to think you’re alone, but I’ve seen encouraging proof that we live in a community filled with caring people.
