About Me

Rick Cedar was born in Boston, Mass and grew up in a Boston suburb. The young Rick grew up an avid reader. He devoured Hardy Boys and Nancy Drew mysteries. “I am your typical die-hard Red Sox fan and still feel the 2004 thrill of my team coming back to beat the Yankees and sweep the Cardinals to win the World Series.” Rick always dreamed of being a writer. He wrote his first book at the age of nine – a story about a frog, called Frantic Frog.

First Book:

Jojo's Bike Shop

Two wounded teens. One common bond: love.

Jojo … Blonde hair. Blue eyes. Motherless. Trying to save her family’s bike shop after her father suffers a stroke. She has a boyfriend but can’t stop thinking about her new employee.

Damian … Brown hair. Brown eyes. Abandoned. Adopted. Just out of juvenile detention. Former cycling champion. Bike expert. Needs a job. He’s in love with her and she doesn’t know it.

This is the story of two damaged seventeen-year-olds and how love helps them heal.




Second Book:

Raising Buster

Sixteen-year-old BUSTER GREEN has a two-seam fastball that’s faster than any boy’s. After her mother dies, Buster is sent across the country to live with her alcoholic, ex-major league ballplayer father who rejects the daughter he never knew he had.

Her dad doesn’t want her. She misses her California friends. And worst of all, the summer league commissioner won’t let girls play summer baseball. But feisty Buster is hell-bent on changing all this.




Coming Soon:

Quinn All-In

Seventeen-year-old QUINN MCKAY, an only child, is on a roll. He has parents who love him. He’s got a cute girlfriend. He’s a champion rower with college scholarship offers.

After Quinn survives a freak snowstorm that kills his mother and father, he is placed in a foster-care group home. He falls into a deep depression and struggles to manage a gambling addiction. His girlfriend dumps him and he quits rowing, sacrificing his scholarship offers.

One year after the tragedy, Quinn receives shocking news: the woman he thought was his mother wasn’t his biological mother.

Alone in the world and reeling from an identity crisis, Quinn decides the only way he can rebuild his life is to find his birth mother. While falling in love and learning to trust again, Quinn searches for his mother and ends up finding himself.
